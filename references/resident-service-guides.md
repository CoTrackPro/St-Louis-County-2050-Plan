# Resident Service Guides — Self-Service Navigation

Plain-language guides organized by life events, not by department.
Residents don't think in departments — they think in problems. These
guides answer "what do I do?" and "where do I start?" and reduce the
touchpoints, calls, and walk-in visits required.

Use these to generate: web content, chatbot responses, printed handouts,
Qless waiting-room displays, or in-conversation help for county staff.

## 1. Life-Event Guides

### 1.1 "I Just Moved to St. Louis County"

**What you need to do** (in this order):

1. **Property taxes**: Your property tax account transfers with the deed.
   You'll receive a bill in November. Pay at stlouiscountymo.gov/payments.
   Nothing to do right now unless you need to update your mailing address.

2. **Personal property declaration**: If you own vehicles, boats, or
   trailers, you must file a personal property declaration by March 1.
   File online at [URL].

3. **Voter registration**: Update your address with the Board of Elections.
   Do it online at sos.mo.gov/elections — takes 3 minutes.
   Deadline: 4th Wednesday before any election.

4. **Parks & Recreation**: Browse programs at [URL]. County residents
   get priority registration and resident pricing.

5. **Pet license**: If you have dogs or cats, license them through
   Animal Services. Info at [URL].

6. **Trash & recycling**: This depends on which city you live in.
   If you're in unincorporated St. Louis County, contact [provider].
   If you're in a municipality, contact your city hall.

**Common mistake**: Don't go to the county for vehicle registration,
driver's license, or utilities — those are state (dor.mo.gov) and
private providers.

**One link to bookmark**: stlouiscountymo.gov — search for any service.

### 1.2 "I Want to Build or Renovate My Home"

**Before you do anything else**: Check if your property is zoned for
what you want to do. Contact Planning & Zoning at 314-615-2500 or
check online.

**Step-by-step**:

1. **Check zoning** (free, 5 minutes online or 1 call)
   - Is your project allowed in your zone? Any setback or easement issues?
   - If you need a variance, that's a separate process (45+ days).

2. **Get your plans ready**
   - Simple projects (deck, fence): site plan showing dimensions and location
   - Complex projects (addition, basement): architectural drawings
   - Not sure what you need? Use our pre-application checklist at [URL]

3. **Submit your permit application**
   - Online (preferred): [portal URL] — upload plans, pay fees
   - Walk-in: Clayton, NW Crossings, or South County, Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm
   - **Tip**: Online submissions are processed faster because they skip the intake queue

4. **Wait for review**
   - Simple residential: ~5–7 business days
   - Complex residential: ~10–15 business days
   - Commercial: ~20–30 business days
   - Check status anytime at [portal URL]

5. **Get approved (or revise)**
   - Approved → schedule inspection
   - Needs revision → you'll get specific feedback on what to fix

6. **Schedule inspections**
   - Call or use online scheduler
   - Inspector visits during construction at required checkpoints
   - Final inspection → project complete

**Total time (simple project)**: 2–4 weeks if your application is complete.
**Total cost**: Varies by project — fee schedule at [URL].

**Why applications get rejected** (avoid these):
- Missing site plan or dimensions
- Project doesn't match zoning (didn't check first)
- Incomplete forms (missing signature, wrong address)
- Plans don't show code compliance

### 1.3 "I'm Turning 62+ / Senior Services"

**Programs you may be eligible for**:

| Program | What It Does | Who's Eligible | How to Apply |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Senior Tax Freeze** | Freezes property tax at current level | Age 62+, own & occupy home, income below threshold | Apply through Revenue department; forms at [URL] |
| **Senior Recreation Programs** | Fitness, social, arts at reduced/free rates | Age 60+ (varies by program) | Register at parks website or rec center |
| **Aging Services** | In-home help, meals, transportation, caregiver support | Age 60+ or caregiver of 60+ | Call Human Services: 314-615-4800 |
| **Property Tax Payment Plan** | Pay in installments | Any property owner | Contact Revenue |

**Steps**:
1. Call 314-615-4800 and say "I'd like to learn about senior services"
2. They'll do a quick eligibility check across all programs
3. You might qualify for things you didn't know existed

**Common question**: "Do I have to apply for each thing separately?"
Unfortunately, yes, for now. But the Aging Services intake call can
identify everything you're eligible for in one conversation.

### 1.5 "I Want to Start a Business"

**Step-by-step** (for unincorporated St. Louis County):

1. **Check zoning**: Is your location zoned for your business type?
   Call Planning: 314-615-2500. Do this FIRST — a lease doesn't help
   if zoning doesn't allow your business.

2. **Register your business**: LLC/Corp with MO Secretary of State
   (sos.mo.gov). Sole proprietor? File fictitious name.

3. **Get county business license**: Apply through Permits & Licensing.
   Fee varies by business type.

4. **Building/occupancy permit**: If you're building out a space,
   you need permits. If it's an existing space, you need a certificate
   of occupancy change.

5. **Health permits**: Required if handling food, operating a salon, or
   other health-regulated business. Contact Public Health: 314-615-0600.

6. **Sales tax**: Register with MO Dept of Revenue (dor.mo.gov).
   County does NOT handle this.

7. **Fire inspection**: May be required before opening. Contact your
   fire protection district.

**How long does this take?**: 60–120 days from concept to open for a
simple retail/office business. Restaurant or healthcare = longer due
to health inspections and specialized permits.

**Free help**: Small Business Counseling is available through county
partnerships. Check stlouiscountymo.gov for current programs.

### 1.6 "Someone I Love Died"

**In the first few days**:

1. **Death certificate**: The funeral home typically handles this with
   the county Vital Records office. If you need additional copies,
   request through Vital Records at Clayton or online via VitalChek.
   - Walk-in: same day (Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm)
   - Online: 5–7 business days
   - Cost: ~$13 per copy

2. **Property tax**: If the deceased owned property, the property tax
   account needs to be updated. Contact Revenue at 314-615-5000 with
   the death certificate and any probate documents.

3. **Vehicle/personal property**: Personal property declaration needs
   to be updated. Contact Revenue.

4. **If the property transfers through probate**: The Probate Court
   (Circuit Court, not county) handles this.

**You don't need to figure this out alone.** Call 314-615-5000 and say
"a family member passed away and I need help with property and records."
We'll walk you through everything.

### 1.7 "I Was Recently Released from Incarceration"

**Immediate needs** (first 72 hours):

1. **ID Documents**: Get your birth certificate from Vital Records
   (Clayton, Mon–Thu). Expedited processing may be available — ask.
   State ID from MO DOR (dor.mo.gov) — you need birth certificate first.

2. **Emergency shelter**: Contact Human Services: 314-615-4800 or call 211.

3. **Food**: Dial 211 for nearest food pantry. Apply for SNAP at mydss.mo.gov.

4. **Probation/supervision reporting**: Contact Justice Services at
   314-615-4700 within 24 hours of release.

**Within 30 days**:

5. **Benefits enrollment**: Human Services can screen you for all
   eligible county and state programs in one visit.

6. **Employment**: Workforce development programs available through
   Missouri Job Center (jobs.mo.gov) and county partners.

7. **Expungement eligibility**: Missouri law allows expungement of many
   offenses after waiting periods. Free eligibility check available
   through Legal Aid (lsmo.org) or county programs.

**Key contact**: Justice Services reentry support: 314-615-4700.
Ask for reentry services — they can coordinate across departments.

### 1.8 "I Want to Adopt a Pet"

1. **Browse available animals**: Visit the Animal Shelter (address + hours)
   or browse online at [URL].

2. **Visit the shelter**: Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm. No appointment needed.
   Staff will help you find a good match.

3. **Adoption process**: Application, meet-and-greet with the animal,
   same-day adoption in most cases.

4. **Fees**: Include spay/neuter, vaccinations, and microchip.
   Fee schedule at [URL].

5. **After adoption**: All animals are spayed/neutered before or within
   30 days of adoption. County pet license included.

6. **Not ready to adopt?** Foster an animal temporarily — contact the
   shelter about the foster program.

## 3. Step-by-Step Process Maps

### 3.1 Pay Your Property Tax

```
1. Find your bill
   - Mailed in November to property address
   - Look up online: stlouiscountymo.gov → Real Estate Search → enter address

2. Choose how to pay
   ★ ONLINE (fastest): stlouiscountymo.gov/payments
     - Need: parcel number (on your bill) + payment method
     - Takes: ~2 minutes
     - Confirmation: instant email receipt
   
   ○ BY MAIL: Check or money order to [address]
     - Allow 5–7 business days
     - Include payment stub from bill
   
   ○ IN PERSON: Clayton, NW Crossings, or South County
     - Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm only
     - Join Qless virtual line to save time

3. Deadlines
   - Full payment: December 31
   - Installment option: 1st half Dec 31, 2nd half March [date]
   - Late = penalty + interest. Pay on time.
```

### 3.2 Get a Birth or Death Certificate

```
1. Determine which record you need
   - Birth certificate: for someone born in St. Louis County
   - Death certificate: for someone who died in St. Louis County
   - Other MO counties: Contact that county's recorder
   - Other states: Contact that state's vital records office

2. Choose how to get it
   ★ ONLINE: Order through VitalChek (vitalchek.com)
     - Takes: 5–7 business days by mail
     - Cost: ~$15 + processing fee
   
   ○ IN PERSON: Vital Records at Clayton HQ
     - Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm
     - Bring: Photo ID + relationship documentation
     - Takes: Same day
     - Cost: ~$13 per copy

3. Who can request
   - The person named on the certificate
   - Parent / legal guardian (birth)
   - Spouse / next of kin (death)
   - Legal representative with court order
   - Funeral director (death — typically handled automatically)
```

## 5. FAQ — Top 25 Resident Questions

1. **What are your hours?** Mon–Thu 8am–3:30pm. No Friday service. West County is closed.
2. **Where do I pay my property taxes?** Online at stlouiscountymo.gov/payments, by mail, or walk-in.
3. **When are property taxes due?** December 31 (or installment plan available).
4. **Do I need a permit?** For structural, electrical, plumbing, or mechanical work — yes. For cosmetic — no.
5. **How long does a permit take?** 5–30 business days depending on project type.
6. **Where do I get a birth certificate?** Vital Records at Clayton, or online via VitalChek.
7. **How do I register to vote?** Online at sos.mo.gov/elections. Takes 3 minutes.
8. **Where is my polling place?** Look up at sos.mo.gov/elections/voterlookup.
9. **How do I adopt a pet?** Visit the Animal Shelter Mon–Thu. Browse animals online first.
10. **How do I apply for the Senior Tax Freeze?** Contact Revenue or download the application at [URL].
11. **How do I report a pothole?** Call Transportation: 314-615-8500 or use online form.
12. **How do I file a code complaint?** Contact Planning & Zoning: 314-615-2500.
13. **How do I get a business license?** Apply through Permits & Licensing.
14. **Where do I get a marriage license?** Circuit Court — NOT the county. Go to stlcountycourts.com.
15. **How do I check my property value?** Online Real Estate Search at stlouiscountymo.gov.
16. **How do I appeal my property assessment?** File with Board of Equalization during appeal window.
17. **Where do I register my vehicle?** State DMV (MO Dept of Revenue), NOT the county.
18. **How do I get SNAP/food stamps?** State program — apply at mydss.mo.gov.
19. **What parks programs are available?** Browse all at [parks URL]. 70+ parks and rec centers.
20. **How do I join the Qless virtual line?** Visit [URL], scan QR code at location, or ask staff.
21. **Can I do this online?** Probably. Tax payments, permit applications, vital records, voter registration, and parks registration are all available online.
22. **My trash wasn't picked up — who do I call?** Your municipality (city), not the county. Unincorporated: [provider].
23. **Where do I report animal cruelty?** Animal Services: 314-615-0650.
24. **How do I get help with rent or utilities?** Human Services: 314-615-4800 or dial 211.
25. **What's that RECA thing on the website?** The Federal Radiation Exposure Compensation Act — if you or a family member was affected by nuclear contamination in the St. Louis area, you may be eligible. See stlouiscountymo.gov for details and the claim form.

## 6. Accessibility & Language Access

### For Residents with Disabilities
- **Physical access**: All three open government centers are ADA accessible.
  Parking, entrance, restrooms, and service counters are accessible.
- **Communication access**: Sign language interpreters available on request
  (48-hour notice preferred). Call 314-615-5000.
- **Digital access**: County website targets WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
  If you have trouble using the website, call for assistance.
- **Service animals**: Welcome at all county facilities.
- **Reasonable accommodations**: Request any accommodation by calling
  314-615-5000 or emailing [ADA coordinator].

### For Residents Who Speak Languages Other Than English
- **Interpreter services**: Available by phone at all service points.
  Tell any county employee "I need an interpreter" and they will connect one.
- **Common languages available**: Spanish, Bosnian, Vietnamese, Arabic,
  Mandarin, and many others through phone interpreter service.
- **Translated forms**: Some forms available in Spanish. Check [URL]
  or ask at any service location.
- **You have the right** to receive county services in a language you
  understand. We will never turn you away because of language.
