## 1. County Overview

- **Area**: 523 square miles
- **Population**: ~979,595 residents (ACS estimate)
- **Home Ownership Rate**: 69%
- **Median Household Income**: $67,432
- **Governance**: County Executive (elected) + 7-member County Council
- **Incorporated Municipalities**: 88
- **Unincorporated areas**: Served directly by county government

## 2. Government Structure

### Executive Branch
The County Executive administers county affairs, appoints department heads,
and oversees all departments and committees.

### Legislative Branch
The 7-member County Council proposes, passes, and amends local laws and
approves the annual budget.

### Major Departments

| Department | Primary Functions |
|---|---|
| Administration | County operations, HR, facilities |
| Revenue | Tax collection, assessment, billing |
| Planning | Zoning, land use, comprehensive plan |
| Public Works / Transportation | Roads, signals, fleet, snow removal |
| Parks & Recreation | 70+ parks, rec centers, trails, events |
| Police | Law enforcement, community policing |
| Justice Services | Pretrial, probation, diversion |
| Public Health | Inspections, immunizations, WIC, environmental |
| Human Services | CDBG, aging, disability, veterans, homeless |
| Animal Care & Control | Shelter, adoption, field services |
| Vital Records | Birth/death certificates |
| Board of Elections | Voter registration, elections administration |
| Procurement | Purchasing, bidding, contract compliance |
| Information Technology | Systems, infrastructure, digital services |

## 4. Current Service Model

### Channel Strategy
The county is mid-transition from a walk-in-first model to a digital-first
model. The Friday closure and West County closure signal fiscal and staffing
constraints driving this shift.

**Channel pressure points**:
- Qless virtual queue capacity at remaining 3 locations
- Phone hold times during Mon–Thu compressed hours
- Digital literacy gaps in older and lower-income populations
- Interpreter services for non-English speakers

### Qless Virtual Queue
Residents can join a virtual line for various departments, waiting wherever
they choose until their turn. This reduces physical waiting room congestion
and allows better staff time management.

**KPI implications**: Qless provides rich data on wait times, service times,
no-show rates, and peak demand patterns. This data should feed directly into
Customer Service KPIs.

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Nov–Feb**: Property tax payment peak; Revenue Department high volume
- **Mar–Apr**: Personal property declaration deadline
- **May–Sep**: Parks & Recreation peak; permit activity high
- **Election years**: Board of Elections surge staffing

## 5. Open Data & Transparency

The County Government is committed to providing reliable, accessible, and
transparent public data.

### Current Open Data Assets
- Real estate search (property records, assessment data)
- Crime mapping / statistics
- Election results and voter data
- GIS/mapping layers
- Budget documents
- Council meeting agendas and minutes
- Public notices

### Open Data Maturity Opportunities
- Publish KPI dashboards publicly (What Works Cities model)
- Machine-readable budget data (Open Fiscal Data Package)

## 7. Budget Context

### General Context
- Property tax is the primary revenue source
- County faces pension obligations, infrastructure maintenance backlog,
  and competitive labor market for government talent
- Federal funding (ARPA, CDBG, HUD) supplements operating budget
- Procurement is governed by Chapter 107 of county code

### Budget-KPI Connection
Every KPI improvement initiative should identify:
1. **Cost**: One-time and recurring
2. **Funding source**: Operating, capital, grant, shared services
3. **ROI timeline**: When does the investment pay back?
4. **Failure cost**: What is the cost of NOT improving?

### Shared Services Opportunity
With 88 municipalities in St. Louis County, shared services (IT, procurement,
HR, public safety dispatch) represent significant efficiency potential.
The county website highlights regional cooperation as a priority:
"What Does a Stronger St. Louis Look Like?" — inviting residents to share
ideas for improving services through regional cooperation.
