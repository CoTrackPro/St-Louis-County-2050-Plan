## 1. Digital Transformation

### 1.1 Los Angeles County — Online Permit Platform
- **What they did**: Launched a unified online permit portal replacing walk-in-only submission. Included real-time status tracking, online plan upload, and automated notifications.
- **Results**: Online submission rate went from 15% to 72% within 2 years. Average processing time decreased from 21 days to 9 days. Walk-in volume dropped 45%.
- **Source**: LA County Department of Regional Planning annual reports; What Works Cities case study.
- **STL County relevance**: Directly applicable to Permits & Licensing. The county's current ~67% online rate has room to grow with a better portal and staff promotion (CS-B1).

### 1.2 San Diego County — Digital Self-Service Portal
- **What they did**: Built a resident-facing portal organized by life events (not departments). Included eligibility screeners, document checklists, and appointment scheduling.
- **Results**: Walk-in visits reduced by 35% in year one. Customer satisfaction increased from 3.4 to 4.1 out of 5. Call volume dropped 22%.
- **Source**: San Diego County HHSA digital transformation report; GovTech coverage.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for the resident service finder and life-event guides in this skill.

## 2. Stat Programs

### 2.1 Baltimore CitiStat (the original)
- **What they did**: Mayor O'Malley launched biweekly data-driven meetings where department heads presented KPIs and defended performance. Every action item tracked and followed up.
- **Results**: Overtime spending reduced by $6M. Pothole repair time dropped from weeks to days. Absenteeism reduced 30%.
- **Source**: Behn, Robert D. "The PerformanceStat Potential" (Brookings Institution, 2014). Harvard Kennedy School case studies.
- **STL County relevance**: The Stat meeting framework in improvement-playbook.md Section 4 is directly based on CitiStat. The key lesson: relentless follow-up on action items matters more than perfect data.

### 2.2 Louisville LouieStat
- **What they did**: Adapted the CitiStat model with public-facing dashboards. Made KPI data available on the city website in real time.
- **Results**: Pothole repair time: 9 days → 3 days. Unscheduled overtime reduced 24%. Public trust in government increased measurably.
- **Source**: Bloomberg Philanthropies What Works Cities certification; Louisville Metro open data portal.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for the public transparency dashboard (Phase 3 of change management rollout).

### 2.3 Chattanooga ChattaData
- **What they did**: Performance management system with public dashboards, linked to budget process. Departments justify budget requests with KPI performance data.
- **Results**: Won What Works Cities Gold certification. Budget decisions became data-informed, not just political.
- **Source**: What Works Cities certification reports.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for Phase 3 institutionalization — embedding KPIs in the budget cycle.

## 3. Queue & Wait Time

### 3.1 Clark County, NV — Virtual Queue (QLess Implementation)
- **What they did**: Deployed QLess virtual queue system across county offices. Residents join from phone and wait wherever they want.
- **Results**: Average wait time reduced 40%. No-show rate dropped to <5%. Customer satisfaction scores increased 28%.
- **Source**: QLess customer case studies; Clark County press releases.
- **STL County relevance**: STL County already uses Qless. The opportunity is optimization — appointment scheduling, time-slot nudging, and cross-location load balancing.

### 3.2 Fairfax County, VA — Appointment-Based Service Model
- **What they did**: Shifted from walk-in-first to appointment-first for complex transactions (permits, tax appeals). Walk-ins still accepted but encouraged to book.
- **Results**: Average wait reduced from 45 min to 12 min. Staff utilization improved (less idle time between random arrivals). Customer satisfaction rose from 3.5 to 4.3.
- **Source**: Fairfax County Government annual report; ICMA conference presentations.
- **STL County relevance**: Especially relevant post-Friday-closure. With 20% fewer service hours, appointment scheduling becomes critical.

## 4. Workforce Development

### 4.1 Hennepin County, MN — Competency-Based Workforce Development
- **What they did**: Mapped every job family to competency profiles. Used assessments to identify gaps. Linked training to KPI outcomes (Kirkpatrick Level 4).
- **Results**: First-year turnover reduced from 22% to 14%. Internal promotion rate increased from 48% to 67%. Training ROI measurable for the first time.
- **Source**: Hennepin County HR annual reports; IPMA-HR conference presentations.
- **STL County relevance**: Direct model for the KSAB framework and competency maps in this skill.

### 4.2 King County, WA — Equity-Centered Employee Development
- **What they did**: Integrated equity competencies into all job descriptions, evaluations, and training programs. Required equity lens in every budget request and policy decision.
- **Results**: Established as a national model for government equity practices. Service disparities reduced in multiple departments.
- **Source**: King County Equity and Social Justice Strategic Plan; GARE network case studies.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for the equity competency sequence in workforce-development.md Section 10.

## 5. Constituent Service Design

### 5.1 UK Government Digital Service (GDS) — "Start with user needs"
- **What they did**: Redesigned all national government services around user needs, not department structures. Created GOV.UK as a single entry point organized by life events and tasks.
- **Results**: GOV.UK became the model for digital government worldwide. Transaction completion rates increased 50%+ across services. Won multiple design awards.
- **Source**: Government Digital Service blog (gov.uk/gds); "Digital Transformation at Scale" (Greenway, Terrett, Bracken, 2018).
- **STL County relevance**: The philosophical foundation for the resident service guides and service finder — organize by what residents need, not by how government is structured.

### 5.2 Austin, TX — 311 Unified Service Request System
- **What they did**: Single 311 number and app for all non-emergency city service requests. Automatic routing to correct department. Real-time status tracking.
- **Results**: First-contact resolution increased from 55% to 78%. Referral loops reduced by 60%. Average resolution time decreased 35%.
- **Source**: Austin 311 annual reports; What Works Cities case studies.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for reducing the "referral black hole" handoff failure pattern.

## 6. Equity & Access

### 6.1 Minneapolis — Equity Metrics Dashboard
- **What they did**: Published equity metrics alongside performance metrics. Every KPI disaggregated by race, geography, and income. Disparities >10% required explanation and action plan.
- **Results**: Service disparities identified and addressed in parks, permitting, and public works. Budget allocation adjusted based on equity data.
- **Source**: Minneapolis Racial Equity Dashboard; GARE network.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for equity checks in the scorecard template and district-level reporting.

### 6.2 Philadelphia — Language Access Program
- **What they did**: Mandated interpreter services at all city service points. Translated top 25 forms into 8 languages. Trained all front-line staff on interpreter protocols.
- **Results**: Non-English-speaking residents' service utilization increased 40%. Complaint rate from LEP residents dropped 55%.
- **Source**: Philadelphia Mayor's Office of Immigrant Affairs reports.
- **STL County relevance**: Model for the multilingual service guide in employee-toolkit.md Section 9.

## 7. Animal Services

### 7.1 Jacksonville, FL — No-Kill Achievement
- **What they did**: Implemented foster-to-adopt pipeline, community TNR program, microchip scanning at all intake points, and partnership with rescue organizations.
- **Results**: Live release rate went from 52% to 92% in 4 years. Average length of stay reduced from 21 days to 9 days. Volunteer hours tripled.
- **Source**: Jacksonville Animal Care and Protective Services annual reports; Best Friends Animal Society community program data.
- **STL County relevance**: STL County shelter at 89% live release — within reach of the 90% no-kill benchmark. Foster pipeline and TNR program would close the gap.
