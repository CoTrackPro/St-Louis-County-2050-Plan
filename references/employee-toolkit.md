# Employee Service Delivery Toolkit

Practical tools for front-line county employees: decision trees, call
scripts, referral guides, job aids, and escalation protocols. These
directly improve KPIs by reducing handle time, increasing first-contact
resolution, and eliminating referral loops.

## 1. Service Triage Decision Tree

When a constituent contacts the county (any channel), use this tree to
route them in under 2 minutes. Goal: first-contact resolution or warm
handoff — never a cold "call this number."

```
CONSTITUENT CONTACTS COUNTY
│
├── "I need to PAY something"
│   ├── Property tax → Revenue (online: stlouiscountymo.gov/payments)
│   ├── Permit fee → Permits portal
│   ├── Court fine → Circuit Court (NOT county — redirect to courts.mo.gov)
│   └── Utility bill → Municipality (NOT county — identify their city)
│
├── "I need a DOCUMENT"
│   ├── Birth/death certificate → Vital Records (Clayton, or online VitalChek)
│   ├── Property tax receipt → Revenue (online portal or walk-in)
│   ├── Permit copy → Permits (online portal)
│   ├── Police report → Police Records (precinct or online request)
│   ├── Court record → Circuit Court (CaseNet: courts.mo.gov)
│   └── Marriage license → Circuit Court (NOT county)
│
├── "I need to BUILD/RENOVATE something"
│   ├── Do I need a permit? → Permits (likely yes for structural work)
│   ├── Is my property zoned for this? → Planning & Zoning FIRST
│   ├── I have plans ready → Permits portal (submit online)
│   └── I don't know where to start → Pre-application checklist (Section 8.1)
│
├── "I need HELP / SERVICES"
│   ├── Housing assistance → Human Services
│   ├── Food assistance (SNAP) → State DSS (NOT county — redirect)
│   ├── Senior services / aging → Human Services (Aging division)
│   ├── Senior Tax Freeze → Revenue
│   ├── Domestic violence → Safe Connections (314-531-2003) + county resources
│   ├── Mental health crisis → BHR (Behavioral Health Response, 314-469-6644)
│   ├── Animal complaint → Animal Services
│   ├── Code violation → Planning & Zoning (code enforcement)
│   ├── Pothole / road issue → Transportation (or 314-615-8500)
│   └── Don't know who to contact → Stay on line, I'll find the right person
│
├── "I need to REGISTER / SIGN UP"
│   ├── Voter registration → Board of Elections (or online: sos.mo.gov)
│   ├── Parks program → Parks & Rec (online registration system)
│   ├── Pet license → Animal Services
│   ├── Business license → Permits & Licensing
│   └── Vehicle registration → State DMV (NOT county — redirect to dor.mo.gov)
│
├── "I have a COMPLAINT"
│   ├── About a county employee → HR / department supervisor
│   ├── About a county service → Log in CRM, route to department head
│   ├── About a neighbor (noise, property) → Municipality police OR county code enforcement
│   ├── Animal cruelty / neglect → Animal Services (field response)
│   └── Environmental / health hazard → Public Health
│
└── "I'm not sure what I need"
    → Ask: "Can you tell me a little more about what's going on?"
    → Listen for keywords, then route using tree above
    → If still unclear: "Let me connect you with someone who can help.
       I'll stay on while we find the right person."
```

### Routing Rules
- **Never say "that's not our department."** Say "Let me find the right person for you."
- **Always offer the digital option** before routing to walk-in.
- **If transferring, do a warm handoff** — stay on the line, introduce the constituent, share context.
- **Log every interaction in CRM** within 5 minutes of close.
- **State vs. County confusion is the #1 mis-route.** Vehicle registration, marriage licenses, SNAP, Medicaid, and court records are STATE, not county.

## 2. Phone Scripts

### 2.1 Greeting
```
"Thank you for calling St. Louis County [Department]. This is [Name].
How can I help you today?"
```

### 2.2 Property Tax Payment Call
```
"I can help you with that. Before we look up your account, did you know
you can pay online anytime at stlouiscountymo.gov/payments? It takes
about 2 minutes and you'll get an instant confirmation.

Would you like me to walk you through the online process, or would you
prefer to handle it over the phone today?"

[If online]: Walk through portal steps. Offer to stay on line.
[If phone]: "I'll need your parcel number or property address..."
```

### 2.3 Senior Tax Freeze Inquiry
```
"The Senior Property Tax Freeze is a great program. Let me make sure
you have what you need.

To be eligible, you need to be 62 or older, own and live in the property,
and meet the income threshold. The application is available online at
[URL] or I can mail you one.

Would you like me to check if you might be eligible based on a few
quick questions?"
```

### 2.4 "Where Do I Start?" (Permit Inquiry)
```
"Happy to help! Before you apply for a permit, the first step is checking
that your property is zoned for what you want to do. That takes about
5 minutes.

I can help you check right now if you have your property address. Or,
you can use our online pre-application checklist at [URL] — it walks
you through everything you need before submitting.

What are you planning to do?"
```

### 2.5 Transfer / Referral Script
```
"I want to make sure you get to the right person. Let me transfer you
to [Department/Name]. I'll stay on the line to introduce you so you
don't have to repeat everything.

[To receiving department]: Hi, this is [Name] from [Department]. I have
[Constituent name] on the line. They're calling about [brief summary].
Can you help them from here?"
```

### 2.6 Can't Resolve / Callback Script
```
"I want to make sure we get this right for you. I'm going to [research
this / check with my supervisor / look into the system], and I'll call
you back by [specific time].

Can I confirm your callback number? And the best time to reach you?

[Log in CRM with callback commitment and deadline]"
```

## 3. Walk-In Service Scripts

### 3.1 Qless Greeting (When Constituent Arrives)
```
"Welcome to [Location]. Are you already in the Qless virtual line?

[If yes]: "Great, we'll call your name when it's your turn. Average
wait right now is about [X] minutes."

[If no]: "Let me help you join. You can scan this QR code or I can
add you right here. While you wait, you can leave and come back —
we'll text you when you're next."
```

### 3.2 Walk-In Pre-Screen (Reduce Incomplete Visits)
```
"Before you sit down to wait, let me make sure you have everything
you'll need for your visit.

You're here for [service]. You'll need:
- [Document 1]
- [Document 2]
- [ID / proof of address]

Do you have all of these with you today?"

[If missing]: "I don't want you to have to come back. Here's what you
need — you can get [document] at [location/online] and come back
any time Mon–Thu. Would you like me to add you to Qless when you're
ready so you skip the wait?"
```

### 3.3 End of Visit
```
"Is there anything else I can help you with today?

Before you go — did you know you can [pay taxes / check permit status /
register for parks programs] online at stlouiscountymo.gov? That way
you don't have to come in next time."
```

## 4. Cross-Department Referral Guide

When a constituent's need spans multiple departments, use this guide
to route them efficiently — ideally completing everything in one visit
or one call.

### Common Multi-Department Needs

| Constituent Says | Departments Needed | Best Routing |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| "I want to build a deck" | Planning → Permits → Revenue | Check zoning FIRST, then submit permit online |
| "I just moved here" | Revenue → Elections → Parks | Start at Revenue (property tax setup), mention voter registration and parks programs |
| "I'm turning 65, what do I get?" | Revenue → Human Services → Parks | Start with Senior Tax Freeze (Revenue), refer to Aging Services and senior rec programs |
| "I'm getting out of jail, what help is there?" | Justice → Vital Records → Human Svc | Reentry navigator coordinates; prioritize ID documents and housing |
| "I want to open a restaurant" | Planning → Permits → Health → Revenue | Zoning check → business license + building permit → health inspection → tax setup |
| "My parent needs help at home" | Human Services → Public Health | Aging services assessment → home health referral |
| "I want to adopt a pet and need a fence permit" | Animal Services → Permits | Start at shelter; mention fence permit requirement; offer permit portal link |

### Warm Handoff Protocol
1. **Don't just give a phone number.** Connect them.
2. **Share context.** Tell the receiving department what the constituent needs.
3. **Confirm arrival.** If referring for a future visit, follow up within 48 hours.
4. **Log the referral** in CRM with: date, from-dept, to-dept, constituent ID, reason, confirmed Y/N.

## 5. Escalation Protocols

### When to Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Constituent is upset and I can't resolve | Supervisor (in person or warm transfer) | Immediately |
| I don't know the answer and can't find it | Supervisor or department SME | Within 15 min |
| Constituent threatens harm to self or others | Security + BHR (314-469-6644) | Immediately |
| Constituent reports fraud, waste, or abuse | County Auditor hotline | Same day |
| Media inquiry | Communications / PIO | Immediately (do NOT respond to media directly) |
| Sunshine Law / records request | Supervisor + Records Custodian | Within 1 business day |
| ADA accommodation request I can't fulfill | ADA Coordinator | Within 1 business day |
| Elected official contact | Supervisor + Government Relations | Immediately |

### Escalation Script
```
"I want to make sure you get the best help possible. Let me bring in
my [supervisor / specialist] who has more experience with this.

[To supervisor]: I have [name] here/on the line. They need [summary].
I've already [what you've done]. Can you help from here?"
```

## 6. Digital Self-Service Promotion

**Goal**: Mention the online option in EVERY interaction. Not pushy —
just informational. This is the single biggest driver of the Digital
Self-Service Rate KPI.

### Promotion Scripts by Service

| Service | Script |
|---------|--------|
| Tax payment | "Did you know you can pay online in about 2 minutes at stlouiscountymo.gov/payments? You'll get instant confirmation." |
| Permit status | "You can check your permit status anytime at [portal URL] — it updates in real time." |
| Vital records | "Birth and death certificates can be ordered online through VitalChek. It usually arrives in 5–7 business days." |
| Parks registration | "All our rec programs are available online — you can browse and register at [URL]." |
| Voter registration | "You can register to vote or update your address online at sos.mo.gov/elections." |
| Property info | "Our Real Estate Search tool at [URL] lets you look up any property in the county." |
| Qless | "Next time you need to visit, you can join our virtual line from home at [URL]. We'll text you when it's your turn." |

### The "Next Time" Close
End every in-person or phone interaction with:
```
"Next time you need [this service], you can do it online at [URL].
Would you like me to write that down for you?"
```

## 7. Difficult Interaction Playbook

### De-Escalation Framework (LEAD)

1. **Listen** — Let them finish. Don't interrupt. Acknowledge what they said.
   - "I hear you. That sounds frustrating."
2. **Empathize** — Validate the emotion, not necessarily the complaint.
   - "I understand why that would be upsetting."
3. **Ask** — Clarify what they need (often different from what they're saying).
   - "What would be the best outcome for you today?"
4. **Do** — Take action or explain clearly what you can do.
   - "Here's what I can do right now: [action]. And here's the next step: [step]."

### Common Difficult Scenarios

**"I've been waiting too long"**
```
"I'm sorry about the wait. I know your time is valuable. Let me help
you as quickly as I can. What do you need today?"
[Don't explain WHY the wait exists — just fix it.]
```

**"I was here before and nothing got resolved"**
```
"I'm sorry that happened. Let me look up what happened last time so
we can pick up where you left off and make sure it gets resolved today."
[Look up CRM history — this is why documentation matters.]
```

**"I pay taxes and you people..."**
```
"I understand your frustration. You deserve good service and I want
to help you today. Let's focus on [their specific need]."
[Don't argue. Redirect to the task.]
```

**"I want to speak to your supervisor"**
```
"Absolutely. Let me get [supervisor name] for you. While I do that,
can I get the details of what you need so they can help you right away?"
[Never refuse this request. It's a right, not an escalation failure.]
```

**"This is a Sunshine Law request"**
```
"I'll make sure that gets handled properly. Let me connect you with
our records custodian who handles public records requests. Can you
put your request in writing — email is fine — so we have it documented?"
[Do NOT try to answer or deny. Route to designated custodian.]
```

## 8. Common Questions Quick Answers

### 8.1 Permits & Building

**"Do I need a permit for [X]?"**
- Deck, patio, addition, finished basement: YES
- Fence under 7 feet (check zoning): MAYBE (check local municipality too)
- Interior paint, flooring, cabinets (no structural): NO
- Roof replacement (same footprint): Check — varies
- **When in doubt**: "Let me check for you" → call Permits

**"How long will my permit take?"**
- Simple residential (deck, fence): target 5–7 business days
- Complex residential (addition, basement): target 10–15 business days
- Commercial: target 20–30 business days
- "These are targets — I can give you a more specific estimate once your application is in the system."

### 8.2 Taxes

**"When are property taxes due?"**
- Typically December 31. Bills mailed in November.
- First half can be paid by Dec 31, second half by March (installment option).

**"How do I appeal my assessment?"**
- Contact Board of Equalization during appeal window (typically July–August in reassessment years).
- Must file in writing with specific grounds.

**"Am I eligible for the Senior Tax Freeze?"**
- Age 62+, own and occupy the property, meet income threshold.
- Application period opens annually.
- "Let me check the current year's income threshold for you."

### 8.3 Services That Are NOT County

| What They Ask For | Who Actually Handles It | Redirect To |
|-------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Vehicle registration / plates | MO Dept of Revenue | dor.mo.gov, local license office |
| Marriage license | Circuit Court | stlcountycourts.com |
| Divorce / custody | Circuit Court | stlcountycourts.com |
| Driver's license | MO Dept of Revenue | dor.mo.gov |
| SNAP / food stamps | MO Dept of Social Services | mydss.mo.gov |
| Medicaid | MO HealthNet | mydss.mo.gov |
| Unemployment | MO Dept of Labor | labor.mo.gov |
| Trash pickup (in a municipality) | The municipality, not the county | "Which city do you live in?" |
| Water / sewer | Missouri American Water or municipality | Check by address |
| Electric / gas | Ameren | ameren.com |

## 9. Multilingual Service Guide

### Language Access Protocol
1. **Ask, don't assume**: "Do you prefer to speak in another language?"
2. **Use the interpreter line**: [Phone number/vendor — e.g., LanguageLine]
3. **Don't rely on family members** to interpret — especially children.
4. **Common languages in STL County**: Spanish, Bosnian, Vietnamese, Arabic, Mandarin
5. **Translated materials**: Check [intranet location] for forms in other languages.

### Interpreter Call Script
```
[To interpreter line]: "I need a [language] interpreter for a
[government service type] call. The constituent is
[on the phone / in person]."

[To constituent]: "I'm getting an interpreter to help us. It will just
take a moment."
```
