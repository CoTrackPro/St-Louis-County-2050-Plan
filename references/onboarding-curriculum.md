# Onboarding Curriculum Sequencer — 90-Day New Employee Development

Maps KSAB competency development into a sequenced 90-day onboarding
curriculum for new county employees. Competencies are introduced in
dependency order — foundational knowledge before applied skills,
attitudes shaped through early experiences, behaviors established
through supervised practice.

Load `references/workforce-development.md` for competency definitions.

## 1. Sequencing Principles

### The KSAB Dependency Chain
Competencies must be developed in order. You can't build Skills without
Knowledge, and Behaviors don't stick without Attitudes.

```
Week 1–2: KNOWLEDGE FOUNDATION
  ↓  "What do I need to know?"
  ↓  Regulations, policies, systems, department context
  ↓
Week 2–4: SKILL BUILDING
  ↓  "How do I do the work?"
  ↓  Shadowing, guided practice, system training
  ↓
Week 3–6: ATTITUDE SHAPING
  ↓  "Why does this matter?"
  ↓  Constituent stories, mission connection, peer culture
  ↓
Week 4–12: BEHAVIOR ESTABLISHMENT
     "Do I do it consistently, under pressure, without supervision?"
     Supervised solo work → independent work → feedback → reinforcement
```

### Pacing Rules
- Never schedule more than 4 hours of formal training in one day for front-line staff
- Alternate knowledge intake (reading, eLearning, classroom) with practice (shadowing, guided work)
- Build in reflection time — new employees need to process, not just absorb
- Week 1 should feel welcoming, not overwhelming. Administrative and cultural orientation first
- First solo work should be low-stakes with a safety net

### Assessment Checkpoints
- **Day 5**: Knowledge check — does the employee understand department mission, core processes, and systems login? (Informal, conversational)
- **Day 14**: Skills check — can the employee perform 3 core tasks with guidance? (Observed task)
- **Day 30**: First formal check-in — review all KSAB domains, identify early gaps, adjust plan
- **Day 60**: Intermediate check — can the employee handle routine work independently? (Supervisor assessment)
- **Day 90**: Graduation — comprehensive assessment against role competency profile. Transition to ongoing IDP.

## 2. Universal County Onboarding (All Employees)

Every new county employee completes this baseline before department-specific training.

### Week 1: Orientation & Foundation

| Day | Morning (9am–12pm) | Afternoon (1pm–3:30pm) | KSAB Domain |
|-----|-------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Day 1** | Welcome & admin (badge, parking, IT setup, benefits enrollment) | County overview: mission, structure, 14 departments, County Executive/Council roles | K |
| **Day 2** | HR policies: ethics, harassment prevention, Sunshine Law, records retention | Department tour: meet team, understand physical space, find key resources | K |
| **Day 3** | Cybersecurity awareness training (mandatory) | Core systems training: email, intranet, time entry, leave requests | K + S |
| **Day 4** | Constituent service orientation: "Who we serve" — county demographics, equity principles, ADA/language access | Observe experienced colleague serving constituents (shadow 2 hours) | K + A |
| **Day 5** | Department-specific orientation begins (see role curricula below) | Meet with supervisor: discuss 90-day plan, set expectations, assign buddy/mentor | A |

### Mandatory Compliance Training (Complete within 30 days)

| Training | Format | Duration | Deadline |
|----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Sexual harassment prevention | eLearning | 1 hour | Day 14 |
| Ethics and conflicts of interest | eLearning | 45 min | Day 14 |
| Cybersecurity awareness | Classroom (Day 3) | 1 hour | Day 3 |
| ADA and reasonable accommodations | eLearning | 30 min | Day 21 |
| Records retention and Sunshine Law | eLearning | 30 min | Day 21 |
| Workplace safety (OSHA basics) | eLearning | 30 min | Day 30 |

## 3. Role-Specific Curricula

### 3.1 Customer Service Representative

**Target competencies**: CS-K1, CS-S1, CS-S2, CS-S3, CS-A1, CS-A2, CS-B1, CS-B2
**Critical first 90 days because**: This role is the front door. Mistakes = constituent frustration + wrong referrals + rework for other departments.

#### Week 1 (after universal orientation)

| Day | Focus | Activities | KSAB | Competency |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Day 5 | County services catalog — Part 1 | Study guide: what each department does, hours, eligibility basics. Quiz prep. | K | CS-K1 |

#### Week 2: Knowledge Deep Dive

| Day | Focus | Activities | KSAB | Competency |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Mon | Services catalog — Part 2 | Finish services guide. Knowledge quiz (target ≥80%). | K | CS-K1 |
| Tue | Qless system training | Hands-on: create queues, manage flow, run reports. Practice with test accounts. | S | CS-K2 |
| Wed | Phone system training | ACD login, transfer protocols, hold procedures, call documentation. | S | CS-S2 |
| Thu | Shadow experienced rep (full day) | Observe walk-in service, phone calls, Qless management. Take notes. | K + A | CS-A1 |
| Fri | No in-person service (closed) | Self-study: referral pathways guide. Reflection journal entry. | K | CS-K3 |

#### Week 3: Guided Practice

| Day | Focus | Activities | KSAB | Competency |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Mon | Triage simulation | Role-play: 10 scenarios, practice routing to correct department in <2 min. | S | CS-S1 |
| Tue | Phone: supervised calls | Take live calls with experienced rep listening/coaching. Debrief after each. | S + B | CS-S2, CS-B2 |
| Wed | Walk-in: supervised service | Serve walk-in constituents with buddy present. Focus on greeting, triage, documentation. | S + B | CS-A1, CS-B2 |
| Thu | Language access & interpreter services | Practice using interpreter line. Review multilingual resources. | S | CS-S3 |
| Fri | No in-person service | Review week: what went well, what questions remain. Adjust plan. | — | — |

#### Week 4: Solo with Safety Net

| Day | Focus | Activities | KSAB | Competency |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Mon–Thu | Independent service with buddy nearby | Handle walk-in and phone independently. Buddy available for questions but not hovering. Supervisor observes 2 interactions/day. | B | All CS |
| Fri | Day 30 check-in | Formal 30-day assessment: knowledge re-quiz, supervisor observation rating, self-reflection. | — | — |

#### Weeks 5–8: Expanding Competence

| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Digital self-service promotion | Learn online portal features; practice guiding constituents to digital options in every interaction | B (CS-B1) |
| Cross-department shadowing | Spend 2 hours each in Revenue, Permits, and Parks to understand what happens after referral | K |
| Difficult constituent scenarios | De-escalation workshop (2 hours). Role-play angry/confused/frustrated callers. | S |
| CRM documentation quality | Supervisor reviews 10 CRM entries; feedback on completeness, clarity, follow-up flags | B (CS-B2) |

#### Weeks 9–12: Independence & Assessment

| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Full independent operation | No buddy required. Supervisor spot-checks 1 interaction/day. | B |
| First-contact resolution focus | Track personal FCR rate. Identify top 3 referral patterns that could be resolved at first contact. | B |
| Day 60 check-in | Intermediate assessment: FCR rate review, CSAT for served constituents, CRM audit. | — |
| Day 90 graduation | Full KSAB assessment against CS competency profile. Transition to ongoing IDP. | — |

**Day 90 success criteria**:
- Services catalog knowledge quiz ≥90%
- FCR rate ≥65% (building toward 75% target)
- CRM documentation compliance ≥90%
- Supervisor observation rating ≥3 on all CS competencies
- Digital self-service offered in ≥80% of observed interactions

### 3.2 Permit Plan Reviewer

**Target competencies**: PL-K1, PL-K2, PL-S1, PL-S3, PL-A1, PL-A2, PL-B1, PL-B2
**Critical because**: Technical expertise takes months to build. Early mistakes create backlog and rework.

#### Weeks 1–2: Foundation
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Zoning code overview | Self-study: county zoning ordinance chapters 1–6. Mentor Q&A sessions (2 × 1hr). | K (PL-K1) |
| Building code orientation | Residential building code study guide. Focus on most common permit types (deck, basement, addition). | K (PL-K1) |
| Permit system training | Hands-on: navigate application queue, status updates, reviewer assignment, note entry. | S (PL-S3) |
| Fee schedule and exemptions | Study fee schedule. Practice calculating fees for 5 sample applications. | K (PL-K2) |

#### Weeks 3–4: Shadowing
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Shadow senior reviewer (full days) | Observe plan reviews. Senior reviewer narrates decision process aloud. New hire takes notes. | K + S (PL-S1) |
| Attend 2 pre-application meetings | Observe how experienced staff guide applicants through requirements. | A (PL-A2) |
| Practice reviews (non-production) | Review 5 previously-decided applications. Compare decisions to senior reviewer's actual decisions. Discuss discrepancies. | S (PL-S1) |

#### Weeks 5–8: Guided Reviews
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Simple residential reviews (supervised) | Review live applications — simple residential only. Senior reviewer co-signs all decisions. | S + B (PL-S1, PL-B1) |
| Written feedback practice | For each rejection, draft written feedback with specific fix path. Senior reviewer edits and coaches. | B (PL-B2) |
| Code interpretation workshop | 4-hour facilitated session: ambiguous code scenarios, gray areas, when to escalate. | K (PL-K1) |
| Customer interaction training | 2 hours: how to explain rejections without being adversarial. Empathy + clarity. | A (PL-A1) |

#### Weeks 9–12: Building Independence
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Independent simple residential reviews | Review and decide without co-signature. Spot-check by supervisor (20% sample). | B (PL-B1) |
| Introduction to complex residential | Shadow complex reviews. Begin guided practice on additions/major renovations. | S (PL-S1) |
| Day 90 assessment | Code knowledge test (≥85%). Review quality audit (accuracy, completeness, turnaround). Supervisor KSAB rating. | — |

**Day 90 success criteria**:
- Code knowledge test ≥85%
- Simple residential reviews completed independently at ≤7 day average
- Written feedback provided on 100% of rejections
- Supervisor observation rating ≥3 on PL competencies
- Zero constituent complaints directly attributed to new reviewer

**Note**: Full proficiency for plan reviewers typically takes 12–18 months.
The 90-day plan establishes foundation and independent capability for
simple residential permits. Complex residential and commercial review
continue developing through Year 1 IDP.

### 3.3 Revenue / Tax Service Agent

#### Weeks 1–2: Foundation
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Missouri property tax law overview | Study guide + mentor sessions. Assessment cycle, payment timelines, delinquency rules. | K (RT-K1) |
| Tax system navigation | Hands-on: look up parcels, view payment history, process payments, issue receipts. | S (RT-S3) |
| Senior Tax Freeze process | Eligibility criteria, application workflow, documentation requirements. | K (RT-K2) |
| Payment portal walkthrough | Learn online portal from constituent perspective. Practice guiding callers through it. | S (RT-S3) |

#### Weeks 3–4: Shadowing + Practice
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Shadow experienced agent (phone + walk-in) | Observe payment processing, assessment questions, angry taxpayer de-escalation. | K + S + A |
| De-escalation training | 3-hour workshop: role-play upset taxpayer scenarios. Empathy techniques. | S (RT-S2) |
| Supervised payment processing | Process live payments with buddy present. Zero-error tolerance on amounts. | S + B (RT-B1) |

#### Weeks 5–8: Independence Building
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Independent phone service | Take tax season calls independently. Promote online payment in every call (tracked). | B (RT-B1, RT-B2) |
| Error audit | Supervisor audits 10% of transactions weekly. Target: <1% error rate. | B (RT-B1) |
| Senior Freeze application processing | Handle applications with supervisor review on first 10, then independently. | S + B |

#### Weeks 9–12: Proficiency
| Focus | Activities | KSAB |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Full independent operation | All payment types, all channels. | B |
| Appeal process familiarity | Observe Board of Equalization hearing. Understand referral process. | K |
| Day 90 assessment | Knowledge test (≥85%). Error rate audit. Online payment promotion rate. Supervisor KSAB rating. | — |

### 3.4 Abbreviated Curricula (Other Roles)

For these roles, follow the same Week 1–2 (Knowledge) → Week 3–4 (Shadowing/Skills) →
Week 5–8 (Guided Practice) → Week 9–12 (Independence) pattern. Department-specific
competency maps in `references/workforce-development.md` Section 3 define what to train.

**Parks & Recreation Program Coordinator**:
- Week 1–2: Safety standards, first aid/CPR cert, facility operations, registration system
- Week 3–4: Shadow program delivery, observe maintenance workflows
- Week 5–8: Lead programs with experienced co-facilitator, practice reservation system
- Week 9–12: Independent program delivery, day-90 assessment
- Special: CPR/AED certification must be complete by Day 30

**Animal Services Officer**:
- Week 1–2: Animal handling safety, shelter management system, intake procedures, county ordinances
- Week 3–4: Shadow field responses and shelter operations
- Week 5–8: Supervised field responses, guided intake processing
- Week 9–12: Independent field work, day-90 assessment
- Special: Bite protocol and rabies quarantine procedures by Day 14

**Justice Services Case Manager**:
- Week 1–2: Supervision conditions, risk assessment tools (ORAS/LSI-R), trauma-informed practice principles, HMIS/case system
- Week 3–4: Shadow experienced case managers, observe court proceedings
- Week 5–8: Carry reduced caseload with supervisor co-signature on all decisions
- Week 9–12: Full caseload with weekly supervision, motivational interviewing practice
- Special: Motivational interviewing workshop by Day 45

**Police Officer (Post-Academy)**:
- Week 1–2: Department general orders, use-of-force policy, body camera procedures, precinct orientation
- Week 3–12: Field Training Officer (FTO) program — structured phases per department FTO manual
- Special: Crisis intervention training within first year; community engagement ride-alongs by Day 60
- Note: Police onboarding is heavily regulated by POST Commission. Curriculum must comply with state requirements.

## 4. Onboarding KPIs

Track these to measure onboarding program effectiveness:

| KPI | Definition | Target | Frequency |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 90-day assessment pass rate | % of new hires meeting all Day 90 success criteria | 85% | Per cohort |
| Time to independent operation | Days from start to supervisor clearing employee for independent work | Role-dependent (see curricula) | Per hire |
| New hire satisfaction | Onboarding experience survey at Day 30 and Day 90 | 4.0/5.0 | Per hire |
| First-year turnover rate | % of new hires who leave within 12 months | <20% | Quarterly |
| Buddy/mentor satisfaction | Buddy/mentor rates the program's support for their role | 4.0/5.0 | Per pairing |
| Knowledge quiz improvement | Score gain from Day 5 baseline to Day 90 | ≥30 percentage points | Per hire |
| Supervisor confidence rating | "How confident are you in this employee's readiness?" (1–5) | ≥4 at Day 90 | Per hire |

## 6. Buddy/Mentor Program Structure

### Buddy (Peer — First 90 Days)
- Same department, same or adjacent role
- Not the supervisor (creates safety for questions)
- Responsibilities: answer daily questions, model norms, introduce to colleagues, provide emotional support
- Time commitment: ~30 min/day in weeks 1–2, ~15 min/day weeks 3–12
- Buddy receives: 1-hour training, small recognition (gift card, time off)

### Mentor (Senior — First 12 Months)
- Same department, senior role or subject matter expert
- May be supervisor or not
- Responsibilities: technical guidance, career advice, knowledge transfer, code/policy interpretation
- Time commitment: 1-hour meeting biweekly
- Mentor receives: included in IDP as "mentoring others" development activity

### Selection Criteria
- Buddy: Willing, patient, good communicator, 1+ year tenure, good performance
- Mentor: Deep expertise in new hire's role area, 3+ years tenure, track record of developing others
- Neither should be assigned involuntarily — quality depends on willingness

## 7. Common Onboarding Failures

| Failure | Symptom | Root Cause | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----|
| "Sink or swim" | New hire on phones Day 2 with no training | No structured curriculum, staffing pressure | This document. Protect onboarding time even when short-staffed. |
| "Death by PowerPoint" | 3 straight days of classroom lectures; new hire retains nothing | All Knowledge, no Skills practice | Alternate K and S activities. Max 4 hours formal training/day. |
| "Shadow forever" | New hire shadows for 6 weeks, never does anything independently | No defined transition points | Assessment checkpoints with explicit "begin supervised solo" triggers |
| "Compliance only" | Onboarding is just mandatory eLearning; no department-specific development | HR owns onboarding but department doesn't participate | Department-specific curriculum starts Day 5. Supervisor accountable for 90-day plan. |
| "No buddy" | New hire sits alone at lunch, doesn't know who to ask questions | Buddy program not implemented or buddy too busy | Assign buddy before start date. Buddy's schedule cleared for Week 1. |
| "The vanishing supervisor" | Supervisor does Day 1 welcome then disappears until Day 90 review | No check-in cadence built into plan | Mandatory check-ins at Day 5, 14, 30, 60, 90. Calendar holds set before start. |
| "Culture shock" | Employee from private sector stunned by government pace, bureaucracy, constraints | No realistic job preview during hiring | Honest job preview in interview. Day 1 orientation covers government context, constraints, and purpose. |
