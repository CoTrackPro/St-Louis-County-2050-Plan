Use this template to create a personalized development plan for a county
employee, linking their KSAB growth to department KPIs and career progression.
Works for both performance improvement and professional growth contexts.

## 6. Check-In Schedule

| Date | Check-In Type | Focus | Participants |
|------|-------------|-------|-------------|
| [Date — Month 1] | 30-day | Confirm activities underway, remove barriers | Employee + Supervisor |
| [Date — Month 3] | 90-day | Progress on Goal 1, early behavior indicators | Employee + Supervisor |
| [Date — Month 6] | Mid-year | Formal review of all goals, adjust if needed | Employee + Supervisor + HR |
| [Date — Month 9] | 9-month | Pre-assessment, identify remaining gaps | Employee + Supervisor |
| [Date — Month 12] | Annual | Full reassessment, plan renewal/graduation | Employee + Supervisor + HR |

### Check-In Discussion Guide

At each check-in, cover:
1. **Progress**: What development activities were completed since last check-in?
2. **Evidence**: What observable changes in knowledge/skill/attitude/behavior?
3. **Barriers**: What's getting in the way? (time, resources, access, support)
4. **KPI connection**: Has the linked KPI moved? Any contribution visible?
5. **Adjustments**: Do goals, activities, or timelines need to change?
6. **Recognition**: What's going well? Acknowledge growth.

## Completion Instructions

1. Employee and supervisor complete collaboratively — not top-down.
2. Link every goal to a department KPI where possible.
3. Activities must span the correct KSAB domain (don't prescribe training for behavior gaps).
4. Include both performance-linked and career-growth goals.
5. Establish check-in cadence before plan begins — accountability is critical.
6. For performance improvement plans: coordinate with HR on documentation
   requirements and civil service procedures.
7. All illustrative proficiency ratings must be labeled `[ILLUSTRATIVE]`.
